Which countries may United States travelers visit?

See every country that travelers from the United States may visit, according to data provided by hundreds of United States embassies internationally—updated every few hours. This website exists to fill the lack of a good, centralized source of information on open countries that is well-formatted (with tables and maps) and not out-of-date.

Please note that the CDC does not recommend travel, especially when it is non-essential. Always take precautions when traveling such as wearing your mask and avoiding large gatherings. Never travel when you know you are sick. For more information on the COVID-19 pandemic, please visit the CDC's COVID-19 information page.

  • Open to Travel
    • Open: The country allows United States citizens to fly in, regardless of purpose (e.g. tourism).
    • Partially Open: The country only allows United States citizens to enter for specific purposes (e.g. returning to home, international school, etc).
    • Mostly Closed: Only specific people are granted access to the country (e.g. diplomats, corporate leaders).
    • Closed: No entry to the country is permitted by the country's government.
  • Quarantine
    • Not Required: The country will not require you to quarantine on arrival. Note: Although not required, a quarantine is recommended. When in public, always wear a mask to avoid unnecessary exposure. Do not travel if you know you are sick.
    • Required: The country has specified that you will be required to quarantine on arrival. Check the URL to see if there are any relevant exceptions (e.g. through a COVID-19 test).
  • COVID Test
    • Not Required: The country has not specified any COVID-19 testing requirements. Note: Although not required, a COVID-19 test is still recommended prior to departure. Do not travel if you know you are sick.
    • Required: The country has specified COVID-19 testing requirements to enter the country. See the URL for more info.

Our data is sourced from international United States embassies. Unfortunately, there is no standard for how the information we use is given to us. Although we do our best to interpret the information, sometimes, our automated system is sometimes unable to interpret the non-standard information the embassies provide. To avoid displaying false information, we instead display "UNKNOWN" for the data we are uncertain about. We instead provide the URL for you to manually check or verify any entry.

Note that "SEE URL" is similar to "UNKNOWN" in that it requires you to check the URL. When we display "SEE URL", our systems detected multiple different answers to the same question ("is the country open?"). This is usually due to the fact that some countries pass the responsibility of determining whether or not to stay open to states, territories, or even cities.

Although we have data for the vast majority of countries, there are a few that the United States has not established an embassy in. For that reason, we do not have a data source to collect information for every country from.

We're sorry we didn't provide you with high quality data! We'd love to get a chance to improve our algorithms or at least not display false information. Shoot us an email at contact@opencountrieslist.com and we will do our best to improve.
